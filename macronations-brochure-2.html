Expect the unexpected

Crisis events test your resilience

On rounds 3, 6, and 9 a random crisis may strike your economy. Your response determines how much damage you take.

🏦
Banking crisis
Major banks face insolvency. Choose: bail them out, nationalise, or let them fail. Each option has different fiscal and political costs.
🦠
Pandemic outbreak
A major pandemic disrupts labour markets and trade flows. Emergency spending vs. targeted support vs. minimal intervention — your call.
⚔️
Global trade war
Trading partners impose severe tariffs on your exports. Negotiate, retaliate, or absorb the shock — each path carries real consequences.
📉
Sovereign debt crisis
Bond markets lose confidence. Choose between IMF bailout with austerity, debt restructuring, or default — with escalating long-run damage.

Season structure

A full season, start to finish

Here's what the typical MacroNations season looks like from registration to champion.

📋
Phase 1
Registration opens
Tournament is published with entry fee, schedule, and prize pool projection. Teams register, pay entry fee, and prepare strategy. One team per player per season year. Teams can have 1–5 members.
🚀
Phase 2
Regular season (5–10 rounds)
Weekly rounds open and close on a fixed schedule. Each round you submit 10 policy decisions and a written rationale. The simulation engine runs, crises fire, scores update. Live rankings visible at all times.
⚡
Phase 3
Playoff bracket
Top 8 teams from the regular season qualify for the playoff bracket. Seeded matchups (1v8, 2v7, etc.) produce a single-elimination bracket. Playoff qualifiers earn a special certificate.
🏆
Phase 4
Champion crowned
The final standings determine the champion. Cash prizes are distributed within 7 business days. All certificates are issued. The champion team enters the permanent Hall of Champions at macronations.com.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How is the simulation different from a real economy?
MacroNations uses simplified versions of real economic models — the same ones taught in universities — but with parameters tuned for a 10-round competitive format. Real economies are far more complex. The simulation is designed to reward genuine macroeconomic reasoning, not memorising tricks.
Do I need to study economics to compete?
No formal economics training is required. The AI policy advisor gives contextual recommendations based on your economy's current state each round. That said, players with economics backgrounds will have an edge on the strategic nuances — which is part of the learning.
How long does a tournament last?
Tournaments run 5–10 rounds with weekly deadlines, so typically 5–10 weeks for the regular season plus 1–2 weeks for playoffs. The time commitment per round is about 15–20 minutes to review your economy and set your policy decisions.
How are prizes paid out?
Winners are contacted within 7 business days of tournament completion. Prizes are paid via bank transfer or Stripe to verified accounts. Winners are responsible for any applicable taxes on prize winnings. MacroNations LLC issues a 1099-MISC where required by US law.
What if I miss a round deadline?
If you miss a round, your economy runs on default policy values for that round. This will hurt your score but won't disqualify you. Consistently missing rounds will significantly impact your final standing — the simulation rewards active engagement.
Can professors run their own tournament?
Yes. Professors can register as instructors, create a class tournament with a custom schedule, set a $0 entry fee, and share the tournament link with students. You get full admin access to manage teams, advance rounds, and view all policy decisions and scores — useful for grading and class discussion.
Are entry fees refundable?
Entry fees are non-refundable except as required by applicable law. Fee waivers are available at the admin's discretion for financial hardship cases. Free tournaments are always available alongside paid competitions.
